### Chapter One What Is Management?

#### THE ONE-LINE DEFINITION OF A MANAGER’S JOB
- The management aspect has nothing to do with employment status and everything to do with the fact that you are no longer trying to get something done by yourself. ([Location 271](https://readwise.io/to_kindle?action=open&asin=B079WNPRL2&location=271))
- This is the crux of management: It is the belief that a team of people can achieve more than a single person going it alone. It is the realization that you don’t have to do everything yourself, be the best at everything yourself, or even know how to do everything yourself. Your job, as a manager, is to get better outcomes from a group of people working together. ([Location 279](https://readwise.io/to_kindle?action=open&asin=B079WNPRL2&location=279))
#### HOW DO YOU TELL A GREAT MANAGER FROM AN AVERAGE MANAGER?

The first criterion looks at our team’s present outcomes; the second criterion asks whether we’re set up for great outcomes in the future. #### THE THREE THINGS MANAGERS THINK ABOUT ALL DAY
- I’ve come to think of the multitude of tasks that fill up a manager’s day as sorting neatly into three buckets: purpose, people, and process. ([Location 322](https://readwise.io/to_kindle?action=open&asin=B079WNPRL2&location=322))

- The first big part of your job as a manager is to ensure that your team knows what success looks like and cares about achieving it. ([Location 333](https://readwise.io/to_kindle?action=open&asin=B079WNPRL2&location=333))
- The next important bucket that managers think about is people, otherwise known as the who. Are the members of your team set up to succeed? Do they have the right skills? Are they motivated to do great work? ([Location 338](https://readwise.io/to_kindle?action=open&asin=B079WNPRL2&location=338))
- Finally, the last bucket is process, which describes how your team works together. ([Location 344](https://readwise.io/to_kindle?action=open&asin=B079WNPRL2&location=344))
- Who should do what by when? What principles should govern decision-making? ([Location 346](https://readwise.io/to_kindle?action=open&asin=B079WNPRL2&location=346))
- In a team setting, it’s impossible for a group of people to coordinate what needs to get done without spending time on it. ([Location 354](https://readwise.io/to_kindle?action=open&asin=B079WNPRL2&location=354))
- Your role as a manager is not to do the work yourself, even if you are the best at it, because that will only take you so far. Your role is to improve the purpose, people, and process of your team to get as high a multiplier effect on your collective outcome as you can. ([Location 380](https://readwise.io/to_kindle?action=open&asin=B079WNPRL2&location=380))
