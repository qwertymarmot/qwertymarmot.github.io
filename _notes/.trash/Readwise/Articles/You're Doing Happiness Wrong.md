## Highlights
- The nature of happiness is fleeting, dancing in the periphery. The moment you think you have a firm grasp on it, happiness slips out of your hands.
- Happiness is obtainable; you might just have to change up your approach.
- The nature of happiness is fundamentally paradoxical. In truth, the pursuit of happiness does not ultimately lead to happiness.
- Happiness always follows a meaningful pursuit, which could entail engaging with hardship and adversity.
- However, once you make happiness your main pursuit, it will always remain elusive.
- Happiness must happen, and the same holds for success: you have to let it happen by not caring about it.
- Pleasure is fleeting. It does not produce long-lasting, life-enriching satisfaction.
- What does bring true joy, as I said above, is the pursuit of meaning, purpose, and significance.
- If you're not sure what your purpose is, think of what burdens you. What do you see as a problem in the world that needs fixing? Climate change? Bullying? Poverty? Human trafficking?
- Consider that your burden may be your calling. If you are open to that idea, what strengths and skills do you possess, or could you possess, that could help solve the problem?
- What's stopping you from throwing your hat into the ring and getting involved?
- Sure, you, as an individual, may not be able to do much, but if you joined an organization? There is power in collective action. You, lending your energy and skills to a group, can accomplish much!
- Toys, wealth, possessions, and career success are drops in the bucket compared to meaningful, nurtured relationships.
- Investments in people yield the greatest return.
- “Career Crafting,” which is the idea that you can infuse a meaningful purpose into your career that transcends the job description.
- People take great pleasure when they are working at something which involves their strengths and passions.
- "The good life is using your signature strengths every day to produce authentic happiness and abundant gratification." —Martin Seligman
