## Highlights
- Modernism is the assumption that the world is clearly-defined and measurable. There are facts that exist independently of any of us. Gravity will always be gravity. Two plus two will always be four. ([View Highlight](https://instapaper.com/read/1360222799/14538022))
- Postmodernism, on the other hand, believes that certainty is impossible. No matter how many times you observe something, you can never know if it’s entirely true, mostly because the observer is always fallible. ([View Highlight](https://instapaper.com/read/1360222799/14538025))
- Einstein’s relativity showed that, in fact, gravity is not always gravity ([View Highlight](https://instapaper.com/read/1360222799/14538029))
- Heisenberg’s Uncertainty Principle showed that it’s theoretically impossible to measure anything with complete accuracy. ([View Highlight](https://instapaper.com/read/1360222799/14538033))
- To put it succinctly, modernism is the assertion that truth can be known definitively. Postmodernism is the assertion that truth can never be known definitively; it can only be guessed at and approximated, at best. ([View Highlight](https://instapaper.com/read/1360222799/14538038))
- Postmodernists believe that people’s feelings matter more than evidence—as evidence is always corrupted by the individual narratives that present it ([View Highlight](https://instapaper.com/read/1360222799/14538054))
- Modernists believe in the sanctity of cultural traditions. Postmodernists see these traditions as evidence of oppression. Modernists believe in protecting institutions. Postmodernists believe institutions merely serve the elites who run them. Modernists believe in the accumulation of scientific knowledge. Postmodernists are skeptical of science, as numbers can be easily manipulated to fit groups’ narratives. ([View Highlight](https://instapaper.com/read/1360222799/14538060))
- It’s no surprise then that the internet has brought a rapid rise in postmodernist thinking around the world. ([View Highlight](https://instapaper.com/read/1360222799/14538063))
- In constantly being exposed to the world’s flaws and foibles, we lose faith in everything and everyone. Knowledge itself becomes politicized. ([View Highlight](https://instapaper.com/read/1360222799/14538069))
- Postmodernism is the reason why everything seems to be becoming politicized these days, even if it shouldn’t be. It’s why academic journals rescind scientific research because some groups may find it offensive. It’s why corporations have begun to adopt slogans that have absolutely nothing to do with their products and please only a tiny fraction of their customers. It’s why politicians who lost elections claim the vote was fraudulent without any evidence. It’s why public figures unironically claim they have “alternative facts” as if the term wasn’t an oxymoron. ([View Highlight](https://instapaper.com/read/1360222799/14538075))
- I’ve written many times in this newsletter about the negativity bias—the fact that negative information gets more attention and feels more intelligent and important to us than positive attention. ([View Highlight](https://instapaper.com/read/1360222799/14538094))
- It’s much more exciting to see a documentary showing governmental corruption than governmental policy-making. It’s far more entertaining to read about how diets are scams rather than understand actual nutritional science. It’s more engaging to learn about a leader’s private failings rather than their public successes. Wallace is clear that these trends represented the rising tide of postmodernism in full force. They point to the perpetual fallibility of knowledge, the untrustworthiness of all authority, the fault lines within all human experience. ([View Highlight](https://instapaper.com/read/1360222799/14538100))
- “I find gifted ironists sort of wickedly fun to listen to at parties, but I always walk away feeling like I’ve had several radical surgical procedures. And as for actually driving cross-country with a gifted ironist, or sitting through a 300-page novel full of nothing but trendy sardonic exhaustion, one ends up not only feeling empty but somehow… oppressed.” ([View Highlight](https://instapaper.com/read/1360222799/14538102))
- Postmodernism generates a sense of loneliness because it fosters distrust and distance rather than trust. ([View Highlight](https://instapaper.com/read/1360222799/14538104))
- Ideas that your thoughts can alter the universe or that by simply believing yourself to be successful, you can be successful—these are postmodernist ideas. They are beliefs that facts morph and reshape themselves to fit our feelings. I’ve spent the better part of a decade arguing against this bullshit. ([View Highlight](https://instapaper.com/read/1360222799/14538112))
