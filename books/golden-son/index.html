Golden Son

by Pierce Brown

Read: July 2025

science-fiction political-intrigue space-opera war loyalty

Notes & Thoughts

Golden Son

Golden Son is the second book in Pierce Brown’s Red Rising Saga, and it’s a masterclass in escalation. Where Red Rising was contained to Mars and the brutal Institute, Golden Son explodes into space warfare, political machinations, and betrayals that hurt. This is Empire Strikes Back territory—everything gets bigger, darker, and more complex.

The Evolution

From Student to War Leader

Two years have passed since Darrow graduated from the Institute. He’s now a rising star in Gold society, commanding a fleet in the war between rival factions. But his mission remains the same: infiltrate, gain power, and destroy the Society from within.

The Expanded Universe

The scope broadens dramatically:

  • Space battles - Tactical fleet warfare with real stakes
  • Political intrigue - Maneuvering through the Sovereign’s court
  • Economic warfare - Control of resources and trade routes
  • Multiple fronts - Balancing military, political, and revolutionary objectives

The Core Conflict

The Bellona-Augustus War

Darrow serves under ArchGovernor Augustus (the Jackal’s father) in a war against the Bellona family. This puts him in direct conflict with Cassius, his former friend from the Institute who now knows Darrow killed his brother.

Triple Agent Struggles

Darrow must simultaneously:

  • Convince the Golds he’s one of them
  • Advance the Sons of Ares’ revolutionary goals
  • Maintain his own moral compass amidst increasing compromise

The Complexity of Revolution

The simple clarity of rebellion from Red Rising gives way to murky reality. Darrow realizes that destroying the Society might plunge the system into chaos that kills billions. Revolution isn’t just about tearing down—it’s about what comes after.

Key Developments

The Jackal Ascendant

Adrius au Augustus, the Jackal, emerges as one of the most terrifying antagonists in modern fiction. His intelligence, cruelty, and understanding of human nature make him far more dangerous than any military opponent.

Relationships Under Pressure

  • Sevro - Still Darrow’s most loyal friend, now leading the elite Howlers
  • Mustang - The relationship deepens while becoming more complicated
  • Cassius - From friend to enemy, their confrontations are emotionally devastating
  • Victra - A new ally who becomes crucial to Darrow’s plans

The Slave King Rises

Darrow’s growing reputation among all the Colors, particularly the Obsidians, creates both opportunity and danger. He’s becoming a symbol, which means he can’t simply disappear if things go wrong.

Standout Sequences

The Iron Rain

One of the most spectacular battle sequences in science fiction—soldiers in powered armor dropping from orbit onto enemy positions. Brown’s descriptions make you feel every second of the controlled chaos.

The Gala

Political maneuvering at its finest, where words are weapons and every social interaction carries deadly weight. The tension of social combat proves just as gripping as physical warfare.

The Betrayals

Multiple gut-wrenching betrayals that genuinely shock because Brown has made you care about these characters. The emotional stakes are as high as the political ones.

Themes Deepened

The Price of Power

Every advancement Darrow makes requires compromise. Every victory has a cost. The question becomes: at what point does the cost exceed the benefit?

Friendship and Loyalty

The bonds forged at the Institute are tested by war, politics, and competing loyalties. Some break. Some strengthen. None emerge unchanged.

The Myth of Meritocracy

The Gold society claims to be a meritocracy, but Golden Son reveals how the system is rigged to maintain existing power structures. Talent matters less than connections, resources, and ruthlessness.

War’s Brutality

Unlike sanitized space opera, Golden Son shows war’s true face—the pointless deaths, the strategic sacrifices, the civilians caught in between, and the moral injury to those who fight.

What Makes It Exceptional

The Escalation

Everything that worked in Red Rising is amplified. The action is bigger, the politics more complex, the relationships deeper, and the stakes higher.

Character Growth

Darrow evolves from a talented student into a brilliant commander, but he also becomes more morally compromised, more isolated, and more aware of the weight he carries.

The Ending

One of the most brutal endings in the genre. It reframes everything that came before and leaves you desperate for the next book while reeling from what just happened.

My Thoughts

Golden Son is that rare sequel that surpasses its predecessor. Brown takes everything that worked in Red Rising and expands it while adding layers of political intrigue and moral complexity.

The space battles are spectacular, but what really elevates the book is the character work. Darrow’s relationships—with Sevro, Mustang, Cassius, and even his enemies—feel genuine and earned. When things go wrong (and they go very wrong), it hurts because we’re invested.

The pacing is relentless. Just when you think Darrow has found stable ground, Brown pulls the rug out. The final third is especially brutal, culminating in an ending that respects neither the characters nor the reader’s emotional well-being—in the best possible way.

Favorite Quotes

“I am the Reaper and death is my shadow.”

“Man was born in chains, yet everywhere he bleeds for liberty.”

“I am the monument to all your sins.”

“We’re like plunging horses. Raging through the world. Blind and half mad with fear.”

“They do not understand. I am not their sword. I am not their light. I am their reckoning.”

Notes

This is where the series transitions from YA-adjacent dystopia to full-blown space opera. The political maneuvering rivals the best of it—think Game of Thrones in space with faster pacing.

The Jackal is one of those villains who makes you simultaneously horrified and impressed. His schemes have schemes, and he understands people in a way that makes him nearly impossible to outmaneuver.

Fair warning: the emotional whiplash is real, and the ending will leave you immediately reaching for Morning Star.
